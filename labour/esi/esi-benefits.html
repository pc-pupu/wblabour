MEDICAL BENEFITS

Medical Benefit is the most important of all the benefits available to an Insured Person and his family under ESI. In this state medical benefit is imparted through the Directorate of ESI (Medical Benefit) Scheme under the Department of Labour, Government of West Bengal.

Full medical care is provided to the insured person and his family the day he enters insurable employment. The package of medical benefit consists of primary care, secondary care including hospitalization and tertiary care i.e. Super Specialty services though in house facilities, as well through tie up arrangements with reputed Medical Institutions in the state. Besides above supply of Drugs & Dressings is made free of cost, treatment aids like spectacles, hearing aid, denture etc., limb prosthesis are supplied to beneficiaries free of cost. There is provision of ambulance service, immunisation and vaccination service at all the ESI centres implemented in the state. The ESI (Medical Benefit) Scheme also participates in National Health Programmes like RNTCP for control of TB and HIV/ AIDs control programme.

Primary Care

For imparting primary care there is a net work of 289 Insurance Medical Practitioners (IMPs),41 Service Dispensaries (SDs) and 20 tieup dispensaries covering almost all the districts in the state. The primary care net work provides primary level consultation, supply of medicines, certification to the beneficiaries, primary scrutiny of reimbursement claims.

MATERNITY BENEFIT

This benefit also represents periodical payment in case of confinement/miscarriage/medical termination of pregnancy and sickness arising out of pregnancy. The rate of benefit is double the standard benefit rate, which works out to full average wages and more.

The criteria for payment being that an Insured Woman should have worked for 70 days in 2 consecutive contribution periods immediately preceding the date of confinement, expected date of confinement etc.

The maximum period for which this benefit is admissible is 26 weeks, in case of confinement, 6 weeks, in case of mis-carriage. Additional period of one month benefit is also admissible, in case, there is prolongation of sickness arising out of pregnancy/confinement.

SICKNESS BENEFIT

Under the Act, it represents periodical payment of Cash Benefit made to the Insured Persons in respect of sickness certified by I.M.O.s/I.M.P.s. The rate of Sickness Benefit corresponds to Standard Benefit Rate and the rate at which, such benefit is payable, roughly works out to 70% of the average daily wages at the maximum, which can be availed of by an employee for a maximum period of 91 days in two consecutive benefit periods (one benefit period comprises 6 months normally). In order to be eligible for this benefit, an I.P. has to work for at least 78 days in the corresponding contribution period (one contribution periods consists of 6 months normally).

Extended Sickness Benefit : This benefit is provided to an I.P. for any of the 34 listed specific long-term diseases, like TB, Cancer and other malignant diseases requiring prolonged treatment. The rate of Extended Sickness Benefit works out to about 80% of the average wages at the maximum, which is initially payable for a period of 124 days, in addition to 91 days' of Sickness Benefit, which may be extended to 309 days. In fit cases, Extended Sickness Benefit for an additional period of 330 days is also admissible, on recommendation of the Special Medical Board. A few uncommon diseases, over and above 34 diseases listed, can also qualify for the same benefit under discretion of the competent Medical Authority of E.S.I. Corporation, New Delhi.

Enhanced Sickness Benefit: This benefit is equal to full wages is payable to the insured persons undergoing sterilization for 7days/14days for male and female workers respectively.

DISABLEMENT BENEFIT

Disablement Benefit consists of Temporary Disablement Benefit and Permanent Disablement Benefit.

  1. Temporary Disablement Benefit : From day one of entering insurable employment and irrespective of having paid any contribution in case of temporary disablement, sustained by an employee in course of employment. The IP is eligible for Temporary Disablement Benefit at the rate of about 90% of the, average daily wages at the maximum so long as the disability continues.
  2. Permanent Disablement Benefit: This benefit is paid at the rate of 90% of wage in the form of monthly payment depending upon the extent of loss of earning capacity as certified by the Medical Board.

DEPENDENTS' BENEFIT

It is also periodic in nature, payable to the dependants of an I.P., in the event of death of an I.P. due to Employment Injury or Occupational hazard sustained by him as an employee. The benefit is admissible to living widow(s), dependent widowed mother and children up to the age of 18 years. The rate of Dependants' Benefit is 90% of the average daily wages of an I.P

OTHER BENEFITS TO INSURED WORKERS

VOCATIONAL TRAINING
Under the Scheme, an Insured Person can avail himself of vocational training, in the event of his suffering permanent disability the vocational training provides for know-how in different trades, under recognised Government Institutions to earn gainful employment in future.
PHYSICAL REHABILITATION
In case of physical disablement due to employment injury, limb prosthesis is provided to the IP
OLD AGE MEDICAL CARE
For Insured persons retiring on attaining superannuation or under VRS/ERS and person having to leave due to permanent disability, the insured person and his/her spouse can avail of medical benefit on payment of Rs 120/- per annum
RGSKY (UNEMPLOYMENT ALLOWANCE SCHEME)

The Corporation introduced Unemployment Allowance Scheme (Rajiv Gandhi Shramik Kalyan Yojna) w.e.f. 01.04.05, in pursuance of powers conferred U/s. 19 of the E.S.I. Act, 1948.

Under the Scheme, an Insured Person/Insured Woman going out of employment after being insured three or more years due to closure of the Factory/Establishment, involuntary retrenchment or permanent invalidity, are entitled to :

  • Unemployment Allowance equal to 50% of the wage for a maximum period of two years.
  • Medical care for self and family from ESI Hospitals/ Dispensaries during the period IP receives unemployment allowances.
  • Vocational training provided for upgrading skills- Expenditure on fee/ travelling allowance borne by ESIC
ATAL BEEMIT VYAKTI KALYAN YOJANA
This scheme is a welfare measure for employees covered under Section 2(9) of ESI Act, 1948 in the form of cash compensation up to 90 days, once in a lifetime, to be claimed after three months in one or more spells for being rendered unemployed, provided the employee has contributed not less than seventy eight (78) days in each four consecutive contribution periods immediately preceding to the claim of the relief. The relief shall not exceed twenty five percent of the average earning per day
MONEY ORDER FACILITIES
All Cash Benefits, except the first payment of Permanent Disablement Benefit and Dependents' Benefit, can be received by the beneficiaries through Money Order. The commission/cost of Money Order Form is borne by the Corporation itself.
PREVENTION OF HIV/AIDS, FOR THE BENEFICIARIES OF E.S.I. SCHEME

National AIDS Control Organisation (NACO) was set up with a view to meeting national objectives of prevention/control and eradication/prevention of HIV/AIDS. E.S.I. Corporation, being the premier Social Security Organisation in India and engaged in catering medical needs to the industrial workers, has also come forward in implementing the programme of eradication of HIV/AIDS, among the beneficiaries.

World AIDS Day is celebrated in Manicktala E.S.I. Hospital every year. A day-long activity is undertaken for educating the public, as well as the Hospital Staff on the World AIDS Day. ICTC clinics are functioning in Manicktala and Kamarhati (one for each Hospital) Patients are being screened for HIV test through these Clinics provided counseling and referred to ART centers where follow up is done.
